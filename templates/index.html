Disease Prevention Guide

Learn effective strategies to prevent plant diseases and maintain healthy crops.

General Prevention Principles

Proper Watering

Water at the base of plants, avoid wetting leaves, and ensure good drainage.

Adequate Sunlight

Ensure plants receive proper sunlight and air circulation.

Regular Pruning

Remove dead, diseased, or damaged plant parts immediately.

Sigatoka Prevention

Key Prevention Strategies

  • Improve drainage systems to reduce standing water
  • Increase plant spacing for better air circulation
  • Remove infected leaves immediately and destroy them
  • Apply preventive fungicide treatments during wet seasons

Xanthomonas Prevention

Key Prevention Strategies

  • Use disease-free seeds and planting materials
  • Implement crop rotation with non-host plants
  • Sanitize tools and equipment between plants
  • Apply copper-based bactericides preventively

Disease Causes & Risk Factors

Understanding the root causes of plant diseases helps in developing effective prevention strategies.

Environmental Factors

High Humidity

Humidity above 80% creates ideal conditions for fungal spore germination.

High Risk

Temperature Extremes

Both very high and low temperatures stress plants and reduce immunity.

Medium Risk

Poor Air Circulation

Stagnant air increases humidity around plants.

Low Risk
